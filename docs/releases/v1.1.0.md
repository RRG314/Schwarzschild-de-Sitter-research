This release adds a practical tool layer to the repository without blurring the line between exact SdS theory and applied engineering work.

The new tools borrow structure from the Schwarzschild-de Sitter program, but they are benchmarked and promoted only when they show real practical value.

## Why this release matters

- The repository now has a usable applied layer in addition to the theory and workbench lanes.
- Supported SDS-inspired tools are separated from experimental ones.
- The workbench includes a dedicated Tools tab that explains what survived and why.
- The practical layer is documented honestly rather than being folded into the exact-theory story.

## Honest scope

This release does not claim that SdS physics literally governs optimization or scheduling.

What it does claim:
- some SdS-inspired control ideas are practically useful enough to keep
- weak or noncompetitive tools were not promoted
- the theory layer and applied layer are now cleanly separated

What remains open:
- larger-task validation of the supported controller and scheduler
- whether the monitor can become a better predictor than simpler baselines
- whether the one-parameter family or equilibrium stopping earn promotion on stronger workloads
