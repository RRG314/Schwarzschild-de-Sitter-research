Understanding the Explorer
Schwarzschild–de Sitter (SdS) spacetime describes a universe with both a black hole and a cosmological constant (like dark energy). For any fixed value of Λ, the entire family of valid spacetimes is parameterized by a single number: the ratio x = r_b/r_c, where r_b is the black hole horizon radius and r_c is the cosmological horizon radius.
Moving the slider above moves you through this entire family — from nearly pure empty space (x→0) to the Nariai limit where the two horizons merge (x→1).
How to read this: Each visualization updates instantly as you move the slider. The Radial Diagram shows the physical structure of spacetime. The Entropy Budget shows how the total de Sitter entropy is split. The Eisenstein Arc is a geometric identity: the moving point always stays on the arc. The Thermodynamics Panel shows the temperatures of each horizon.

Limits & Interpretation
Pure de Sitter Limit (x → 0) ▶

As x → 0, the black hole shrinks to nothing. The static region becomes the entire spacetime. The de Sitter entropy S_Λ approaches the full entropy, while S_b → 0. The black hole temperature T_b formally diverges (this is the Hawking temperature of a minimal black hole), while T_c → 0. The spacetime approaches pure de Sitter space with no black hole.

Small Black Hole Regime (x ≪ 1) ▶

In this regime, the dominant contribution to the entropy budget is the cosmological entropy S_c. The black hole entropy S_b is negligible, and the entropy deficit Δ is also small. The black hole is much hotter than the cosmological horizon, leading to a large Carnot efficiency. This is the regime most relevant to our actual universe (small black holes in an expanding cosmos).

Nariai Limit (x → 1) ▶

At x = 1, the two horizons merge into a single surface at r = 1/√Λ. Both temperatures approach zero: T_b → 0⁺ and T_c → 0⁻. The mass reaches M_Nariai = 1/(3√Λ). The entropy deficit Δ reaches its maximum: Δ/S_Λ → 1/3. The black hole and cosmological entropies are equal: S_b = S_c = S_Λ/3. The Carnot efficiency vanishes.

⚠ Important: The slider shows different spacetimes in the SdS family at fixed Λ. It is NOT showing time evolution. Each point on the slider is a distinct physical spacetime.
